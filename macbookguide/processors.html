Processors

The brain of a computer. Where faster means better

What They Do

Processors are the brain of a computer. They process all the computer information, files, data, programs and allow the computer to work. Without a processor, we would have a box with a screen, a keyboard and mousepad, that do not work or function together. Everytime you turn on your computer, a signal is sent to the processor who process some data and activates certain parts of the computer, like the screen, the keyboard, the lights, and others. Computer processors have been upgrading every year, allowing you to handle more and more programs at a faster speed every time. If you use your computer to only check your emails, to create programs, edit photos or play games, the processor will always be the key factor for how fast your tasks can be completed. If you currently have an old computer or if you have had a computer before, you might have noticed that everything you try to do on that computer runs slower than ever before. The reason why this happens is because technology is moving so fast that all of our files, documents and data are being upgraded to new technologies that require more space, more power, more everything. It might seem odd but it is the reality and the best way to know what processor is the right for you and what you do, is to understand the technology behind each.
